About Prepared for Disaster

This app was built so that you can create a usable, printable emergency preparedness plan for your household in 10 minutes or less. If you are like us, we knew it was a good idea to have a plan in place, but it always seemed daunting to create. We could have made it more elaborate, but our goal was to help lots of people get from no plan to simple plan that's actually useful.

Phillip Nguyen, Joe McClenahan, Rick Stoner and Greg Magdsick started this app in 4 days, during our project week for our Code Fellows 301 course. The repository for this project is here.

More Resources to be Better Prepared

  • Red Cross Types of Emergencies

    • This page from the Red Cross is a great resource where you can find out more information on various disasters; including what to do to prepare for them, what to do during, and what to do after.

  • Ready.gov

    • Ready.gov gives detailed information on what you need to do to be prepared for an emergency. It gives information for a wide spectrum of scenarios and will help ensure you have a solid plan in place.

  • CDC Emergency Preparedness

    • The first two sites have a lot of information, so it can be a bit tough to find exactly what you need. This site has a simple user interface that gives you a detialed list for an emergency kit, helps with making a plan, and provides where you can find more information about local emergency services.

  • Seattle Dept. of Emergency Management

    • On this site you can get information specific to Seattle and its emergency plans. It also includes a list of natural disasters Seattle is at risk of, with a map showing which areas are most vulnerable.
